# Why AI Evals Are an Increasingly Valuable Skill — Hamel Husain
Source: https://hamelhusain.substack.com/p/why-ai-evals-are-an-increasingly

## The core problem: "vibe-check hell"
Teams without evals can't answer:
- Which model should we use?
- Did this prompt change help or hurt?
- Why is the agent failing on this subset of inputs?
- Is our RAG retrieval actually working?

You end up relying on gut feel ("it feels better") rather than measurement.

## The key argument
Existing ML engineering practices CAN transfer to AI engineering — and they're more accessible than teams assume. Evals are the bridge between engineering rigor and AI uncertainty.

## What evals unlock
- Model switching with confidence
- Prompt iteration with signal
- Debugging multi-step failures
- RAG/retrieval optimization
- Knowing when you're done

## Topics the broader eval landscape covers
- Minimum viable eval setups (start before you're "ready")
- Error analysis methodology
- Synthetic data generation for test cases
- Binary (pass/fail) vs graded evaluation
- Annotation best practices
- LLM-as-judge automation + calibration
- Production monitoring vs CI/CD usage
- Specialized workflows: RAG, multi-step agents, agentic systems
- Human handoff evaluation

## Mindset shift
The framing isn't "what metric should I track" — it's "how do I build a feedback loop fast enough to actually iterate?" Evals are a product engineering practice, not a research practice.
